Roundtable Hold

Roundtable Hold: A Safe Haven for Tarnished

Domain Name Option: roundtable-hold.com

Why This Name?

The Roundtable Hold is one of the most iconic locations in the Elden Ring game world. This site name directly references the gathering place where Tarnished gather to access meet NPCs(other tarnished), and upgrade their equipment. By choosing this name, we immediately communicate the site's purpose and appeal to the Elden Ring community.

Logo/Symbol:

This symbol used as the logo is a scared symbol in the games universe, associated with the worship of the erdtree, where the roundtable hold is situated. The symbol is also associated with the golden order, the founders of the roundtable.

Site Purpose

What is Roundtable Hold?

The Roundtable Hold website serves as a comprehensive hub for Elden Ring players seeking information about this crucial in-game location. The site provides detailed information about:

  • NPCs & Merchants: Profiles of all NPCs available at the Roundtable Hold and the services they provide
  • Weapons & Equipment Upgrades: Details on where to upgrade weapons and spirit ashes,
  • Quest Information: NPC quests and character storylines
  • Access Guide: Multiple methods to unlock and reach the Roundtable Hold in-game
  • Strategic Information: Tips for making the most of the location as a hub for progression

Audience Scenarios

These scenarios represent questions and needs from our target audience of Elden Ring players:

Scenario 1: New Player Finding the Location

"I've heard about the Roundtable Hold in Elden Ring, but I'm not sure how to access it. What do I need to do to unlock this location?"

Solution: Our site provides multiple access methods, from early-game routes to late-game alternatives, so players can find the approach that fits their current progress.

Scenario 2: Seeking NPC Information

"I want to know more about the NPCs at the Roundtable Hold. What do they sell? What are their quests?"

Solution: Detailed NPC profiles with information about their services, questlines, dialogue options, and item availability help players plan interactions and discover hidden stories.

Scenario 3: Equipment Upgrade Planning

"I need to know where to upgrade my weapons and what materials I need. Can I do it all at the Roundtable Hold?"

Solution: Comprehensive guides about the blacksmith, upgrade materials, and spirit ash enhancement help players efficiently manage their equipment progression.

Scenario 4: Item and Loot Discovery

"What valuable items can I find or purchase at the Roundtable Hold? Is it worth exploring thoroughly?"

Solution: A complete database of items, armor sets, weapons, and unique treasures available at the location helps players understand what's available and where to find it.

Color Schema

The Roundtable Hold features a muted, dark fantasy color palette defined by sepia browns, charcoal grays, and weathered stone. These earthy tones are contrasted by soft, golden light radiating from the central table and deep crimson from the carpets in the main hall.

Primary: Charcoal Gray (#2A2A2A)

Usage: Main background color, deep shadows, page header foundation

Accent: Soft Golden Light (#C9A876)

Usage: Section headings, links, borders, emphasis elements representing the glow from the central table

Secondary: Weathered Stone (#9C9591)

Usage: Section backgrounds, boxes, secondary headings, navigation elements

Text Light: Pale Cream (#E8DCC8)

Usage: Primary text on dark backgrounds, body text, readability

Accent: Deep Crimson (#8B3A3A)

Usage: Special emphasis, accent borders, representing the deep red carpets of the main hall

Accent: Sepia Brown (#5C4A3D)

Usage: Navigation elements, subtle backgrounds, representing aged wood and worn materials

Color Application Examples:

  • Soft golden light radiates from the central table, creating warm visual focal points
  • Charcoal grays provide a strong foundation reminiscent of the Hold's dark stone architecture
  • Weathered stone tones add depth and age-worn elegance throughout the design
  • Pale cream text ensures excellent contrast and readability against dark backgrounds
  • Deep crimson accents echo the rich carpet hues found in the main gathering hall

Typography

Carefully selected fonts provide hierarchy, readability, and match the site's theme:

Georgia Serif Font

Font Family: Georgia, serif

Usage: Main headings (h1, h2), section titles, page title

This is a Georgia heading - elegant and classic

Rationale: Georgia provides a classic, noble feel appropriate for a grand location like the Roundtable Hold. Its elegant serifs add sophistication.

Trebuchet MS Sans-Serif

Font Family: Trebuchet MS, Arial, sans-serif

Usage: Body text, paragraph content, navigation, smaller headings (h3, h4)

This is body text in Trebuchet MS - clean and modern for easy reading

Rationale: Trebuchet MS is highly readable on screens and provides a modern, clean contrast to the elegant headings. Perfect for content-heavy areas.

Courier New Monospace (Accent)

Font Family: Courier New, monospace

Usage: Special sections, code blocks (if needed), quotes, special callouts

> This is Courier for special emphasis

Rationale: Used sparingly for special information boxes or technical details to create visual distinction and draw attention.

Typography Hierarchy:

  • H1 (Georgia, 2.5em): Page title "Roundtable Hold - Website Plan"
  • H2 (Georgia, 2em): Main section headings (Site Name, Purpose, etc.)
  • H3 (Trebuchet, 1.5em): Subsection titles within main sections
  • H4 (Trebuchet, 1.2em): Content subsections and scenario titles
  • Body (Trebuchet, 1em): All paragraph text and general content
  • Small (Trebuchet, 0.9em): Navigation, metadata, and secondary information
